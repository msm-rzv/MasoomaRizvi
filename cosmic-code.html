COSMIC-CODE ACADEMY

MAY 2024 - SEPTEMBER 2024

"Cosmic-Code Academy is an informational platform designed for high school students interested in learning Python. The website introduces visitors to the Academy’s course offerings, provides key details about the learning experience, and serves as a way for students and parents to get in touch or express interest."

TOOLKIT

Figma | wireframing & prototyping
HTML, CSS, JavaScript | front-end development
Git, GitHub | project hosting & version control

READ-TIME

5 minutes

"High school students and their parents require an engaging and informative platform to learn about the Python courses Cosmic-Code Academy is hosting."

DISCOVERY

I conducted surveys with high school students (aged 14–18) and their parents to better understand their needs and preferences when selecting a tutoring program.

“It's crucial to know the learning outcomes and how the program aligns with my child's academic goals.” – Hussain, 47

“It's important for me to know the goals of the program and what I'll be able to achieve by the end of it.” – Inayah, 16

“A FAQ section would be helpful to quickly find answers to common questions about the program.” – Sumayyah, 45

“I'm more likely to enroll in a program when the website is visually engaging and interactive.” – Sarah, 17

Competitive Analysis

A competitive analysis revealed:

  1. Poor Navigation: Confusing and unintuitive site structures.
  2. Lack of Clarity: Hard-to-find course information and unclear learning goals.
  3. Misplaced Priorities: Overemphasis on pricing before building value.
  4. Inconsistent UX: Design and content lack cohesion, causing confusion.

DEFINE

Key Pain Points Identified::

  1. Lack of clear course information and outcomes.
  2. Unengaging and outdated design.
  3. Confusing navigation and content overload.
  4. Lack of trust and direct contact options.

Goals:

  1. Simplify Navigation.
  2. Provide Clarity and Transparency.
  3. Enhance UX.
  4. Build Trust and Reputation.

Design & Branding Decisions

Name Selection

The name Cosmic-Code Academy was chosen to capture the imaginative and limitless world of coding through a cosmic lens. The word “Cosmic” evokes a sense of exploration and discovery, while “Code” grounds the brand in its educational focus—teaching Python to high school students. Together, the name is futuristic, inspiring, clearly conveying a sense of learning with limitless possibilities.

Logo & Navigation Design

The logo features a shooting star paired with the name “Cosmic-Code Academy,” reflecting a space-inspired theme. The shooting star symbolizes ambition, progress, and creativity—key traits we want to associate with coding and learning. The navigation bar is clean and minimal, designed to ensure easy access to course information. Typography uses Orbitron, a modern, tech-inspired font that reinforces the coding and futuristic theme. It’s visually bold and easy to read, making it ideal for a high school audience. Hover effects and smooth transitions enhance interactivity, making the site more engaging and fun to explore.

Color Palette

The Cosmic-Code Academy color palette blends vibrant pinks, purples, white, and black to create a bold yet fun cosmic atmosphere. These colors appeal to high school students and visually connect with the space theme. Purple and pink tones reflect imagination and energy, while black and white provide balance and readability. The overall effect is modern, inviting, and designed to spark curiosity.

DELIVER

After building the high-fidelity prototype for Cosmic-Code Academy, I conducted informal usability testing with 4 high school students from the target audience:

  • 2 students with prior interest in Python
  • 1 beginner with no coding experience
  • 1 parent evaluating the site for their child

Goals of Testing

  • Evaluate clarity of course information and layout
  • See how easily users could navigate and find essential details like cost, location, and how to enroll
  • Identify any confusion or friction in understanding the course structure or purpose of the site

Key Feedback + Iterations

“I love the colors, but I wasn’t sure where to click to get more info.”

“The course details were there, but kind of easy to miss or too general.”

“The hover effects were cool—it felt fun, like a game or space app.”

“I viewed it on my phone and some things didn’t line up properly.”

Usability Testing

Based on this feedback, the following updates were made:

  • Reworded and repositioned course info to make it clearer and more visible.
  • Refined language around how to enroll, making the email contact method more direct.
  • Improved spacing and layout for mobile responsiveness.
